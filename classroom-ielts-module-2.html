Module Objectives

  • Differentiate Section 1–4 task styles.
  • Use signpost words and note-taking symbols.

Module 2 Quick Start

  • Preview first: Scan the question set and underline keywords before audio starts.
  • Track signposts: Listen for transitions such as first, however, finally, and in contrast.
  • Control distractions: Expect speakers to change details and confirm final answers.
  • Review errors smartly: Record wrong answers by question type for targeted practice.

IELTS Listening: Complete Guide

The IELTS Listening test lasts about 30 minutes and includes four recordings that get progressively more challenging. You will answer a total of 40 questions.

Section Structure

  • Section 1: Everyday social conversation (e.g. booking arrangements) — factual details.
  • Section 2: Monologue in social context (e.g. local event speech).
  • Section 3: Educational or training conversation (e.g. student–tutor discussion).
  • Section 4: Academic monologue (e.g. lecture). Denser content, signpost tracking essential.

Scoring

Each correct answer earns one mark. Your raw score out of 40 converts to an IELTS band score. There is no penalty for wrong answers — always make an educated guess.

Key Tips

  • Scan questions before each recording to predict answers.
  • Focus on dates, names, numbers, and places.
  • Take quick notes — audio plays once only.
  • If you miss an answer, keep going immediately.
  • Check spelling — incorrect spelling can lower your score.

Practice

  • Complete 3 short recordings and write a one-line summary for each section.
  • Run one 20-minute timed drill (mixed question types) with no replay.
  • Create an error log: spelling errors, distractors, and missed signposts.
  • Repeat 10 weak items and explain why the correct option is right.
