  • Read by meaning: Match ideas and paraphrases, not only exact words.
  • Prioritize evidence: Underline the line that proves each answer.
  • Analyze patterns: Log repeated errors (TFNG, headings, completion, MCQ).

Reading Accuracy Framework

IELTS Reading gives you 60 minutes for all 3 passages and 40 questions. A smart approach is to skim each passage first for topic flow, then scan for evidence when answering each question. Most wrong answers happen because students match exact words instead of matching meaning (paraphrase).

Step 1: Skim for Structure (2–3 min)

  • Read the title and first sentence of each paragraph.
  • Mark paragraph purpose (definition, example, contrast, result).

Step 2: Map Keywords and Paraphrases

  • Underline names, dates, quantities, and technical terms in questions.
  • Search by meaning, not by exact repeated words.

Step 3: Choose by Evidence

  • Confirm the exact line that supports each answer.
  • For TFNG: False = contradicted; Not Given = absent from text.

Step 4: Time Management

  • Target ~20 minutes per passage.
  • If a question stalls you >90 seconds, mark and move on.

Practice Exercise

Short Passage: Urban planners increasingly design "15-minute cities," where residents can access schools, shops, clinics, and green spaces within a short walk or bicycle ride. Supporters argue this reduces traffic and improves public health. Critics warn that implementation can be uneven if housing costs rise near upgraded neighborhoods.

  1. Multiple Choice: What is the main goal of a 15-minute city?
  2. True/False/Not Given: All residents currently live within a 15-minute walk of essential services.
  3. Sentence Completion: Supporters say the model can reduce ______ and improve health.
Show Practice Answer Key
  • Q1: Improve access to essential services nearby.
  • Q2: Not Given — passage describes the concept, not current universal reality.
  • Q3: traffic.
