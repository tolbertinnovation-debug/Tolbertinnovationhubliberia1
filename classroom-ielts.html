What Band Score Do You Need?

Different goals require different bands — here's what to aim for.

5.0
Modest User
Basic communication, limited complex English. Starting point for most students.
6.0
Competent User
Required by many universities for undergraduate admission.
6.5–7.0
Good User
Most top universities and UK visa requirement. The common target for TIH students.
7.5–8.0
Very Good User
Competitive postgrad applications and professional English use.
8.5–9.0
Expert User
Academic research, native-level professional English settings.

IELTS or TOEFL? Know the Difference.

Both are accepted by international universities — but for different regions and use cases.

🇬🇧 IELTS — You Are Here
  • British & Australian accent
  • Paper or computer-based
  • Band 0–9 scale
  • Accepted in UK, Australia, Canada, India
  • Ideal for TIH scholarship programs
🇺🇸 TOEFL iBT
  • American accent throughout
  • Computer-based only
  • Score 0–120 scale
  • Preferred by US universities
  • Integrated speaking and writing tasks

Academic IELTS is designed for students applying to universities at undergraduate or postgraduate level. General Training is used for work experience, migration, and secondary education visas. Both versions use the same Listening and Speaking tests — only Reading and Writing differ.

Most students complete the full 6-module course in 4–6 weeks when studying 1–2 hours per day. You can go faster or slower depending on your schedule — there are no deadlines and no time limits.

After completing the Mock Exam module (Module 6), you can request a TIH Course Completion Certificate. This certificate demonstrates that you have worked through the full structured preparation program.

Most UK universities require a minimum of Band 6.0–6.5 for undergraduate admission and Band 6.5–7.0 for postgraduate programs. Some competitive programs and universities may require higher. Always check the specific requirements for the course you are applying to.
