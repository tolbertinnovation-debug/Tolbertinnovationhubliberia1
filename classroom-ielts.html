FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about IELTS and our preparation program.

IELTS Academic is required for undergraduate and postgraduate university admissions. IELTS General Training is accepted for secondary education, work experience, and immigration to countries like Canada, Australia, and the UK. Both share the same Listening and Speaking sections; the Reading and Writing tasks differ in content and complexity.
It depends on your target institution or country. Most UK and Australian universities require Band 6.0–6.5 for undergraduate programs and 6.5–7.0 for postgraduate. Canadian immigration typically requires Band 6.0. Medical and law programs usually require Band 7.0 or above. We recommend aiming for at least Band 7.0 to maximize your options.
Most students need 6–12 weeks of consistent daily study (1–2 hours per day) to improve by 0.5–1 full band. Students starting from Band 4.5 may need 3–4 months to reach Band 6.5. Our 8-week study schedule is designed for students with a current band of 5.0–6.0 who want to reach 6.5–7.5.
Yes — 100% free. All six structured modules, downloadable PDFs, vocabulary builder, score calculator, and our study planner are permanently free for all students with no registration required. We offer optional Premium plans for students who want live coaching, AI feedback, and direct tutor access, but everything core is free.
The Overall Band Score is the average of your four component scores (Listening, Reading, Writing, Speaking). The average is rounded to the nearest whole or half band: averages ending in .25 or .75 are rounded down (e.g., 6.25 → 6.0; 6.75 → 6.5). Use our score calculator above to test different combinations.
There is no limit on how many times you can take IELTS. You can book and sit the exam as many times as you need. Each attempt is independent, and universities or visa bodies accept your highest score. IELTS results are valid for two years. We recommend preparing thoroughly before each attempt to maximize improvement between sittings.
Yes. Our entire platform is fully responsive and works on smartphones, tablets, laptops, and desktop computers. All lessons, quizzes, and resources are mobile-optimized. The PDF downloads can be saved to your device and studied offline, which is especially useful for students with limited data access.
The IELTS exam fee in Liberia is approximately $200–$250 USD per sitting, payable to the British Council or IDP IELTS registration center. Tolbert Innovation Hub does not charge any fee for preparation materials. We also provide guidance on exam registration, booking dates, and test center locations through our student support team.
