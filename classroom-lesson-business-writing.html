What Is a Business Report?

A business report is a formal document that presents information, analysis, findings, or recommendations on a specific topic. Reports are used in NGOs, government offices, businesses, and schools to communicate results or proposals to decision-makers. A well-written report gets read, understood, and acted upon.

Standard Report Structure

A professional business report follows this structure: (1) Title Page: Report title, author, organization, date. (2) Executive Summary: A 1-paragraph overview written last, placed first. (3) Introduction: Background, purpose, and scope. (4) Main Body: Sections with headings presenting findings and analysis. (5) Conclusions: Summarize key takeaways. (6) Recommendations: Specific, actionable steps proposed.

Writing Clearly and Formally

Use third person: "The findings indicate..." not "I found...". Be precise: "Sales increased by 23% in Q1" not "Sales went up a lot." Keep it concise - every sentence should add value. Remove filler phrases like "It is important to note that..." Avoid jargon unless your audience will understand it. Use formal language throughout the report.

Headings, Tables, and Visuals

Good reports use structure to guide the reader. Use numbered headings (1.0, 1.1, 2.0) so readers can navigate easily. Tables present comparative data more clearly than paragraphs - each table needs a numbered title above it. Charts make trends visible at a glance. A simple bar chart is almost always clearer than a pie chart with more than 5 segments.

💡 Key Tips

  • Write the executive summary last - once you know exactly what the report contains.
  • Use numbered headings so cross-references like "see Section 2.3" are clear.
  • Have someone outside your team read your report - if they understand it, your audience will too.
  • Spell out abbreviations the first time: "Non-Governmental Organization (NGO)" then use NGO throughout.

📝 Practice Assignment

Write a one-page business report on a real or imaginary topic relevant to you - for example: "A Report on Internet Access Challenges in My Community." Include all sections: Title Page, Executive Summary, Introduction, Findings (2-3 points), and Recommendations (2-3 actionable steps). Keep the body under 500 words.
