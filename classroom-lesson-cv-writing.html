What Is a CV and Why Does It Matter?

A CV is a written summary of your education, work experience, skills, and achievements. It is the most important document in your job search. In Liberia, most employers receive dozens of applications for every position. A strong CV gets you to the interview stage. A weak one goes straight to the rejection pile. Your CV is your first impression before employers ever meet you.

CV Structure and Sections

A professional CV includes: (1) Personal Information: name, phone, professional email, city. Do NOT include your photo unless specifically requested. (2) Personal Profile: 2-3 sentences summarizing who you are, tailored to the specific job. (3) Work Experience: reverse chronological order with achievement-focused bullets. (4) Education: reverse chronological. (5) Skills: relevant hard and soft skills. (6) References: "Available on request" saves space.

Writing Strong Achievement Bullets

Weak: "Responsible for managing social media." Strong: "Grew Facebook page from 500 to 4,200 followers in 6 months through daily content and community engagement." Use the Action + Result format. Start each bullet with a strong verb: Managed, Developed, Increased, Designed, Trained, Coordinated, Reduced, Launched. Include numbers whenever possible. Volunteer work and school projects count if you have limited paid experience.

CV Formatting and Common Mistakes

Rules: Keep to 1-2 pages maximum. Use a clean font (Calibri or Arial) at 10-12pt. Save and send as PDF - never .docx. Common mistakes: spelling errors (a single typo can eliminate your application), using one generic CV for every job (tailor it each time), listing job duties instead of achievements, including irrelevant personal details like height or religion.

💡 Key Tips

  • Tailor your CV for every job - adjust the summary and skills to match each job description.
  • Have at least 2 people proofread your CV before submitting.
  • If you have no paid work experience, include volunteer work, school leadership, and projects.
  • Keep a master CV with all your experience, then cut it down for each specific application.

📝 Practice Assignment

Write or update your CV using the structure from this lesson. Include at minimum: Personal Information, Career Summary (2-3 sentences tailored to a specific job), Work Experience with achievement-focused bullets, Education, and Skills. Save it as a PDF. Ask one trusted person to review it and give feedback on: Is it clear? Are there any errors? Does it look professional?
