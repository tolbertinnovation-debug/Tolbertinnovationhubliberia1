📱 Digital & Phone Communication

Master professional phone etiquette, WhatsApp for business, video call best practices, and the unwritten rules of digital communication in the workplace.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand: Professional Phone Call Etiquette
  • Understand: WhatsApp and Messaging for Business
  • Understand: Video Call Best Practices
  • Understand: Response Times and Digital Communication Norms

Professional Phone Call Etiquette

Phone communication remains essential in Liberian business — many decisions are still made over calls. Professional phone etiquette sets you apart. Before making a business call: identify the best time to call (not during lunch, not too early or late), have your key points ready, and if leaving a voicemail, have your message prepared. Answer calls promptly — within 3 rings where possible.

When answering a business call: greet with your name and organization: "Good morning, Tolbert Innovation Hub, this is Samuel." Speak clearly and at a normal pace. Do not eat, chew, or multitask during a professional call. Take notes — you will forget details if you do not write them down. End calls professionally: summarize any action items, thank the caller, and wait for them to hang up first when speaking with a client or superior.

Cold calling (calling someone who does not expect your call): introduce yourself quickly, state your purpose clearly in one sentence, ask if they have 2-3 minutes to speak, and respect their answer. If they say they are busy, schedule a better time — never pressure someone who said they are unavailable. Missed calls: return missed calls within the same business day, or at least send a message: "I missed your call — I will call back at 3pm."

WhatsApp for Business Communication

WhatsApp is the dominant business communication tool in Liberia and across West Africa. Using it professionally requires following norms that differ from personal messaging. Business WhatsApp messages should be: clear and purposeful (no "haha" or forwarded memes in a client conversation), sent during business hours (7am–8pm at the latest), and acknowledged promptly (do not leave clients "on read" for days).

WhatsApp Business App (free download) provides professional features: a business profile with your address, hours, and description; quick replies for common questions; catalog to display products; away messages when you are unavailable. For any customer-facing business in Liberia, WhatsApp Business is more important than a website for day-to-day communication.

Group message etiquette: professional WhatsApp groups (team groups, client groups) should stay on-topic. Avoid sending irrelevant content (forwards, jokes, religious messages) to work groups unless the group culture explicitly allows it. When adding someone to a group, always ask permission first. Mute groups you do not actively need to monitor but do not want to leave. In project groups, keep a record of decisions made in chat by pinning important messages.

Video Call Best Practices

Video calls (Zoom, Google Meet, Teams, WhatsApp Video) are now standard for interviews, client meetings, and team calls. How you appear on a video call directly affects how professional others perceive you. Technical setup: ensure good lighting (face a window or use a lamp in front of you — never sit with a light behind you, which makes you appear as a silhouette). Use a headset or earphones to reduce echo. Find a quiet place. Test your audio and camera 5 minutes before the call.

Background: choose a clean, professional background. A plain wall is ideal. Use the blur background feature in Zoom or Teams if your environment is busy. Dress professionally from the waist up (at minimum) — you may need to stand up unexpectedly. Position your camera at eye level — looking up at a camera from below is unflattering and creates a sense of power imbalance. Look at the camera, not at the other person's face on screen, when speaking — this creates the impression of direct eye contact.

During the call: mute yourself when not speaking. Use the "Raise Hand" feature in Zoom/Teams to indicate you want to speak. Do not check your phone or browse other tabs — others can tell. For long meetings, use chat for questions and comments rather than interrupting. Join 2–3 minutes early to handle technical issues without delaying the meeting.

Response Times and Digital Communication Norms

Response time expectations vary by channel: Urgent/Same-day channels: phone calls (respond within hours), WhatsApp messages in active conversations (within 2–4 hours during business day). Standard channels: email (respond within 24 hours for non-urgent, 48 hours maximum). If you cannot respond fully, acknowledge receipt: "Received — I will get back to you with a full response by tomorrow afternoon."

Digital communication permanence: treat every digital message as a permanent record. Emails, WhatsApp messages, and texts can be screenshotted and shared. Never write anything in a digital message that you would not want your employer, a client, or a journalist to read. This is especially important when discussing colleagues, clients, or business decisions. A WhatsApp screenshot shared publicly can end careers.

Tone and formality: match the formality of the channel and relationship. Emails to senior management: formal, full sentences. WhatsApp with close colleagues: can be more casual but remain professional. Twitter/LinkedIn posts: public, professional always. Do not use all-caps in messages — it reads as shouting. Avoid excessive exclamation marks!!!! as they reduce credibility. Use emojis sparingly in professional contexts — they can be misread across cultures.

💡 Key Tips

  • Set up WhatsApp Business on a dedicated work number — mixing personal and business on one number creates confusion and unprofessionalism.
  • Always test your audio and video before important video calls — a 5-minute tech problem at the start of an interview creates a bad first impression.
  • Return all professional messages within 24 hours, even if just to acknowledge receipt and promise a full response later.
  • Never use voice notes for business communications unless the relationship explicitly allows it — transcribing voice notes is time-consuming for the recipient.
  • In digital communication, clarity beats brevity — a slightly longer message that is understood beats a short message that requires 3 follow-ups.

📝 Practice Assignment

Conduct a digital communication audit: 1) Review your last 10 WhatsApp messages to a professional contact — were they clear, timely, and appropriately formal? Identify 2 improvements you could make. 2) Prepare a WhatsApp Business profile (if applicable to your work) with a professional profile photo, business description, and at least 2 quick replies for common questions. 3) Set up your video call environment: test your lighting (share the camera preview with a friend and ask for feedback), background, and audio. Write down 3 things you improved based on their feedback. 4) Draft a professional phone script for introducing yourself to a potential employer or client in under 30 seconds.
