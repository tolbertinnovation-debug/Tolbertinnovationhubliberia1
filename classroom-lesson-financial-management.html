Financial Management for Entrepreneurs

Track your money, control your costs, and build a business that stays profitable.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the difference between revenue, profit, and cash flow
  • Set up a simple bookkeeping system for your business
  • Read and interpret a basic income statement
  • Manage startup costs and control business expenses

💰 Revenue vs. Profit vs. Cash Flow

Many entrepreneurs confuse these three critical numbers — and that confusion can sink a business.

TermWhat It MeansExample
RevenueTotal money coming in from salesSold 50 bags at L$500 = L$25,000
Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)Direct cost to make/buy what you soldBought bags at L$300 each = L$15,000
Gross ProfitRevenue minus COGSL$25,000 − L$15,000 = L$10,000
Operating ExpensesRent, salaries, transport, marketingL$6,000/month
Net ProfitGross Profit minus all expensesL$10,000 − L$6,000 = L$4,000
Cash FlowMoney actually in your account right nowYou may show profit but have no cash if customers owe you
⚠️ A profitable business can still fail from poor cash flow. If customers buy on credit and don't pay, you cannot pay your suppliers or staff — even if your income statement looks great.

📒 Simple Bookkeeping System

You don't need expensive software to track your finances. Start with a simple cash book.

Cash Book format:

DateDescriptionIncome (In)Expense (Out)Balance
June 1Opening balanceL$10,000—L$10,000
June 2Sold 10 itemsL$5,000—L$15,000
June 3Paid rent—L$3,000L$12,000

The Golden Rules of Small Business Finance:

  • Separate personal and business money — open a dedicated business mobile money account
  • Record every transaction, no matter how small
  • Reconcile your books weekly — compare cash book to actual cash/mobile money balance
  • Keep all receipts for at least 3 years
💡 Free tools for Liberian entrepreneurs: Use Google Sheets (free, works on phone), Wave Accounting (free app), or simply a physical cash book notebook from the stationery store.

📊 Reading a Basic Income Statement

An income statement (Profit & Loss statement) shows whether your business made or lost money over a period.

SAMPLE MONTHLY INCOME STATEMENT — June 2026 ══════════════════════════════════════════ REVENUE Product Sales L$ 45,000 Service Income L$ 12,000 ───────────────────────────────────── Total Revenue L$ 57,000 COST OF GOODS SOLD L$ 28,000 ───────────────────────────────────── GROSS PROFIT L$ 29,000 (51% margin) OPERATING EXPENSES Rent L$ 5,000 Staff Wages L$ 8,000 Transport L$ 2,500 Marketing L$ 1,500 Utilities (phone/internet) L$ 800 Miscellaneous L$ 500 ───────────────────────────────────── Total Expenses L$ 18,300 NET PROFIT L$ 10,700 ══════════════════════════════════════════

Key ratios to monitor:

  • Gross Margin % = Gross Profit ÷ Revenue × 100 (aim for 40%+ for product businesses)
  • Net Margin % = Net Profit ÷ Revenue × 100 (above 10% is healthy for a small business)
  • Break-even point = Fixed Costs ÷ Gross Margin % (how much you must sell to cover costs)

✂️ Managing Costs and Growing Profit

There are only two ways to increase profit: increase revenue or reduce costs. Most entrepreneurs focus only on sales — the smart ones also control costs.

Cost reduction strategies:

  • Negotiate with suppliers — ask for bulk discounts or extended payment terms
  • Reduce waste — track spoilage, returns, and unsold inventory
  • Share resources — share a market stall, vehicle, or equipment with another entrepreneur
  • Go digital — mobile money instead of cash reduces theft and increases tracking
  • Review "lifestyle expenses" — personal spending disguised as business expense

Revenue growth strategies:

  • Increase prices (if your market allows — even 5% improvement boosts profit significantly)
  • Upsell and cross-sell to existing customers
  • Open a new sales channel (WhatsApp, social media, market day)
  • Introduce a complementary product/service
💡 The 10% Rule: If you can cut costs by 10% AND increase revenue by 10%, your profit can double. Small improvements in both directions compound powerfully.
