What Is the Internet?

The internet is a global network of computers and devices connected together. It allows people to share information, communicate, shop, learn, and do business from anywhere in the world.

When you browse a website, your device sends a request to a server (a powerful computer somewhere in the world), which sends back the page data. This happens in milliseconds.

Browsing and Searching

A web browser (Google Chrome, Firefox, Edge) is the program you use to view websites. The address bar at the top is where you type a website address (URL) like www.tolbertinnovationhub.org.

A search engine (Google, Bing) finds websites for you when you type keywords. Tips for better searches:

  • Use specific words: "nursing jobs in Monrovia" not just "jobs"
  • Add "site:" to search within one website
  • Use quotes for exact phrases: "how to write a CV"

Online Safety Essentials

The internet has risks. Protect yourself:

Phishing: Fake emails or messages pretending to be your bank, government, or a company — asking for your password or personal details. Never click suspicious links.

Strong Passwords: Use at least 8 characters, mixing letters, numbers, and symbols. Never use "123456" or your birthday. Use a different password for each account.

Public Wi-Fi: Avoid logging into bank accounts or entering passwords on public Wi-Fi. Hackers can intercept data on unsecured networks.

Recognizing Scams and Fake News

Common online scams in Liberia and across Africa:

  • Money scams: "You won a prize — send us $50 to claim it." Legitimate companies never ask you to pay to receive a prize.
  • Job scams: "Work from home, earn $500/day." If it sounds too good to be true, it is.
  • Fake news: Verify stories on at least two trusted news sources before sharing.
  • Romance scams: Strangers online who quickly ask for money.
Always pause and verify before clicking, sharing, or sending money.

💡 Key Tips

  • Use HTTPS websites (the padlock icon in your browser) for secure connections.
  • Log out of accounts on shared or public computers.
  • Never share your passwords — not even with friends.
  • Enable two-step verification on your email and social media accounts.
