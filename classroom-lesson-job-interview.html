Before the Interview - Preparation

The interview begins long before you enter the room. Research the organization: know their mission, programs, recent news, and the job description. Employers regularly ask "What do you know about our organization?" - have a confident, specific answer ready. Prepare your answers by practicing common questions out loud. Prepare 3 thoughtful questions to ask the interviewer - this shows genuine interest.

The STAR Technique for Answering Questions

For behavioral questions like "Tell me about a time you solved a problem", use STAR: Situation (briefly describe the context), Task (what was your responsibility?), Action (what specific steps did YOU take?), Result (what was the outcome, ideally with a number or measurable impact?). Keep STAR answers to 90-120 seconds. This structure keeps you focused and impressive.

Common Interview Questions to Prepare

Prepare strong answers for: "Tell me about yourself" (a 90-second professional summary of who you are and why you are here), "What are your strengths?" (choose 2-3 with specific examples, not just "I am hardworking"), "What is your weakness?" (be honest about a real weakness and show how you are addressing it), "Why do you want this job?" (specific reasons tied to the organization mission).

Dress Code, Body Language, and Follow-Up

Dress one level above what is required for the role. In Liberia, business formal (ironed shirt, clean trousers or skirt, polished shoes) is appropriate for most professional interviews. Arrive 10-15 minutes early. Sit upright, maintain eye contact, smile genuinely. Do not cross your arms. Send a thank-you message within 24 hours: "Thank you for the interview. I am enthusiastic about this opportunity."

💡 Key Tips

  • Practice mock interviews with a friend - have them ask you tough questions.
  • Bring a printed copy of your CV and a notepad to every interview.
  • If you do not understand a question it is professional to say "Could you please rephrase that?"
  • Research the salary range for the role before the interview so you are prepared to discuss compensation.

📝 Practice Assignment

Conduct a mock job interview. Ask a friend or family member to interview you for a real job you want. Have them ask at least 5 common questions from this lesson. Record the practice session on your phone. Discuss afterward: What went well? What answers need improvement? Repeat the mock interview a second time implementing the feedback you received.
