Why Public Speaking Matters

Public speaking is one of the highest-value skills you can develop. Whether presenting to a team of 3 or an audience of 300, the ability to communicate clearly and confidently sets professionals apart from their peers. Fear of public speaking is one of the most common fears in the world, but like any skill it can be learned and improved with practice.

Preparing Your Presentation

Know your audience: What do they already know? What do they need to learn? Structure your content in three parts: Opening (hook with a question, fact, or bold statement - tell them what you will cover), Body (2-3 main points with examples), Closing (summarize key points and end with a clear call to action). Practice out loud, not just in your head. Time yourself.

Delivery: Voice, Body, and Eye Contact

Voice: Speak slowly - anxiety makes us rush. Pause after key points to let the audience absorb information. Vary your pitch and pace. Body language: Stand tall, use natural hand gestures, move purposefully. Eye contact: Look at individuals for 2-3 seconds at a time, moving around the room. This builds connection and makes the audience feel you are speaking directly to them.

Handling Nerves and Questions

To manage nerves: take 3 slow deep breaths before you begin, remember the audience wants you to succeed, focus on your message not yourself. For questions: listen to the full question before responding. It is professional to say "That is a great question - I will need to look into that and get back to you." Keep answers to 30-60 seconds each.

💡 Key Tips

  • Record yourself practicing on your phone and watch it back - uncomfortable but extremely effective.
  • Join a local community group or church meeting where you can practice speaking regularly.
  • The more you practice, the less nervous you will feel - exposure is the cure for speech anxiety.
  • Never apologize at the start of a presentation ("Sorry for wasting your time") - it undermines your authority.

Q2. What should you do if you do not know the answer to an audience question?

📝 Practice Assignment

Prepare and deliver a 3-minute speech on a topic you care about - a community issue, a skill you have learned, or a change you want to see in Liberia. Record yourself on your phone. Watch the recording and note: Did you speak too fast? Did you make eye contact? Was your structure clear? Practice again and record a second improved version.
