🤝 Teamwork & Conflict Resolution

What Makes a High-Performing Team?

A team is a group of people working together toward a shared goal. Teams consistently outperform individuals on complex tasks. Research on high-performing teams identifies these key factors: clear shared goals (everyone understands what success looks like), defined roles (each person knows their responsibilities), psychological safety (people can speak up without fear of judgment or ridicule), trust (members follow through on commitments), and open communication (regular, honest dialogue about progress and problems).

Your Role in a Team

Every person has a natural team role: some are idea generators, others are organizers, implementers, or critics. Understanding your role helps you contribute more effectively. Regardless of your role: (1) Meet your commitments - do what you say you will do, when you say you will do it. (2) Communicate early if you face a problem - do not wait until a deadline is missed. (3) Support others - offer help when a teammate is struggling. (4) Give and receive feedback constructively - focus on the work, not the person.

Handling Conflict in a Team

Conflict is normal and even healthy in teams when managed well. It signals that people care enough to disagree. Steps for resolving a conflict: (1) Stay calm - take a moment before reacting emotionally. (2) Address the issue directly and privately with the person involved - not via social media or gossip. (3) Listen fully to their perspective before sharing yours. (4) Focus on the issue, not the person ("The report was submitted late" not "You are irresponsible"). (5) Look for a solution both parties can accept. (6) If needed, involve a neutral third party as a mediator.

Collaboration Across Differences

In Liberia and in global workplaces, teams are increasingly diverse - different backgrounds, languages, religions, ages, and perspectives. Diversity is a strength when managed well: different perspectives lead to more creative solutions, diverse teams catch more errors, and inclusive teams are more resilient. Practical steps: listen actively to perspectives different from yours, avoid assuming others think or feel the same way you do, acknowledge contributions from all team members, and challenge unfair treatment of any colleague.

💡 Key Tips

  • The fastest way to build trust in a team is to do exactly what you said you would do, consistently.
  • Give praise publicly and feedback privately - this protects dignity and builds team morale.
  • In a disagreement, repeat back what the other person said before sharing your own view - this shows genuine listening.
  • A team that celebrates small wins together stays motivated longer.

📝 Practice Assignment

Think of a real team situation you are in or have been in - at school, work, a church group, or a family project. Write a short reflection (1 page) covering: (1) What roles did different people play? (2) Was there any conflict? How was it handled? Could it have been handled better? (3) What would you do differently to make that team more effective? If you are not currently on a team, volunteer to coordinate a small group project this week and reflect on the experience.
