TOEFL or IELTS? Know the Difference.

Both are accepted globally — but for different regions, accents, and university types.

🇺🇸 TOEFL iBT — You Are Here
  • American English accent throughout
  • Computer-based only
  • Score 0–120 scale
  • Preferred by US universities
  • Integrated speaking and writing tasks
🇬🇧 IELTS
  • British & Australian accent
  • Paper or computer-based
  • Band 0–9 scale
  • Accepted in UK, Australia, Canada, India
  • Ideal for TIH scholarship programs

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before you start your TOEFL journey.

TOEFL iBT (Internet-Based Test) is the world's most widely accepted English proficiency exam for university admission. It is scored 0–120 and is primarily used for admission to universities in the United States, Canada, and other English-speaking countries. It tests all four language skills in one session.

TOEFL is entirely computer-based and uses American English throughout. IELTS uses British English, can be taken on paper or computer, and scores on a 0–9 band scale. US universities tend to prefer TOEFL, while UK and Australian universities prefer IELTS. Both are accepted worldwide, but always check your specific university's requirements.

After completing the Full Mock Exam stage, you can request a TIH Course Completion Certificate. This certificate demonstrates that you have completed the full structured TOEFL preparation program.

Most US universities require a TOEFL iBT score of 80–100 for undergraduate admission and 90–110 for graduate programs. Highly competitive or Ivy League-level schools may require 100+. Always check the requirements for each specific program you are applying to.
