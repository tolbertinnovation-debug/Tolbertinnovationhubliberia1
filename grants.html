Civil Society & Nonprofits

Grants for Liberian NGOs & Civil Society Organizations

Liberian nonprofits and civil society organizations can access international funding to support community programs, education, health, and development initiatives.

🇺🇸

United States of America

USAID Liberia Grants

NGO / Civil Society Open to Liberians

USAID maintains an active presence in Liberia and regularly issues Requests for Applications (RFAs) for local NGOs working in education, health, governance, agriculture, and economic growth.

What's Funded

  • Project implementation costs
  • Staff salaries
  • Training & capacity building
  • Monitoring & evaluation

Key Requirements

  • Registered Liberian NGO or CBO
  • Clear development mandate
  • Financial management capacity
  • Strong project proposal
📋 grants.gov / USAID Liberia Mission Apply / Learn More →

West Africa

Open Society Initiative for West Africa (OSIWA)

NGO / Civil Society West Africa

OSIWA funds civil society organizations working on governance, human rights, justice, and accountability in West Africa including Liberia.

What's Funded

  • Program activities
  • Staff
  • Research
  • Advocacy campaigns

Key Requirements

  • Registered NGO in West Africa
  • Focus on open society values
  • Demonstrated track record
🌐 osiwa.org Apply / Learn More →

United Nations — Global

UNDP Small Grants Programme (SGP)

Community Organizations Up to $50,000 Liberia Eligible

The Global Environment Facility Small Grants Programme (GEF SGP) operated by UNDP provides grants up to $50,000 to community-based organizations for environmental and sustainable development projects.

What's Funded

  • Community conservation
  • Sustainable agriculture
  • Renewable energy
  • Climate resilience

Key Requirements

  • Community-based organization or NGO
  • Environmental / development focus
  • Participatory approach
💰 Grant amount: Up to $50,000 Apply / Learn More →

United States Government

Africa Development Foundation (USADF)

Grassroots Organizations Up to $250,000

A US government agency that provides direct grants to African grassroots organizations and social enterprises. Strong focus on youth, women, and underserved communities in Liberia.

What's Funded

  • Organizational capacity
  • Social enterprise development
  • Community programs

Key Requirements

  • African grassroots organization
  • Underserved community focus
  • Clear beneficiary impact

SME & Business Funding

Grants & Funding for Liberian Businesses

Liberian small and medium enterprises (SMEs) can access international grants and funding programs to grow, innovate, and create jobs.

🌍

Pan-Africa

Tony Elumelu Foundation (TEF) Entrepreneurship Programme

Entrepreneurs $5,000 Seed Capital Non-refundable

Africa's leading philanthropy initiative empowering African entrepreneurs. Provides $5,000 non-refundable seed capital, 12 weeks of business training, and access to a network of 18,000+ African entrepreneurs.

What's Funded

  • Business seed capital
  • Working capital
  • Equipment

Key Requirements

  • African citizen
  • 18+ years old
  • Business idea or early-stage business
  • Any sector
💰 $5,000 non-refundable + mentorship + network Apply / Learn More →
🌍

African Development Bank

AfDB — Affirmative Finance Action for Women in Africa (AFAWA)

Women Entrepreneurs Business Financing

AfDB's program to unlock $3 billion in financing for women-owned businesses across Africa, with technical support and capacity building.

What's Funded

  • Business expansion
  • Equipment
  • Working capital
  • Capacity building

Key Requirements

  • Women-owned or led business
  • Registered in Africa
  • Growth potential demonstrated
🌍 African Development Bank Apply / Learn More →

World Bank Group

IFC / World Bank SME Finance

SMEs Business Growth

IFC (World Bank Group) supports SME growth in developing countries including Liberia through grants, advisory services, and access to finance programs.

What's Funded

  • Business development
  • Market access
  • Productivity
  • Technology adoption

Key Requirements

  • Registered SME
  • Growth-oriented
  • Sector varies by program

Germany — GIZ

GIZ Liberia — Business Support Grants

German Funded Business Development

Germany's GIZ implements business development programs in Liberia, offering grants and technical assistance to SMEs in agriculture, trade, and services.

What's Funded

  • Technical assistance
  • Market linkages
  • Capacity building
  • Equipment

  • Priority sectors: agribusiness, services, trade

Innovation & Startup Funding

Grants & Competitions for Liberian Entrepreneurs

Young Liberian entrepreneurs and innovators can compete for international grants, prizes, and accelerator programs.

🇺🇸

United States Government

YALI — Mandela Washington Fellowship

Young Leaders USA Exchange Fully Funded

The Young African Leaders Initiative (YALI) Mandela Washington Fellowship — funded by the US government — brings Africa's best young leaders (18–35) to the US for leadership institutes at top US universities.

What's Covered

  • Full travel
  • Accommodation
  • Program costs
  • Networking with US leaders

Key Requirements

  • African citizen
  • Age 18–35
  • Leader in business, civic, or public management
  • English proficiency
🌐 yali.state.gov Apply / Learn More →
🌏

Jack Ma Foundation / Alibaba

Africa Netpreneur Prize Initiative

Digital Entrepreneurs Up to $1,000,000 prize pool

The Africa Netpreneur Prize supports African entrepreneurs with a share of a $1 million prize pool, mentorship from Jack Ma and Alibaba Group, and a platform to scale their businesses.

Grant Amount

  • Share of $1M prize pool
  • Mentorship from Jack Ma & Alibaba
  • Platform to scale business

Key Requirements

  • African entrepreneur
  • Business operating for at least 1 year
  • Any sector
💰 Share of $1M prize pool Apply / Learn More →
🌍

Global — Hult Prize Foundation

Hult Prize

Students & Young Entrepreneurs $1M Grand Prize

The world's largest student competition for social entrepreneurship, with a $1 million grant for the winning team. Open to university students and young innovators globally including Liberia.

Grant Amount

  • Up to $1,000,000 for winning team
  • Global mentorship & network
  • Media exposure

Key Requirements

  • University student or recent graduate
  • Social enterprise idea
  • Team of 2–4
💰 Up to $1,000,000 Apply / Learn More →

Innovation Tech Entrepreneurs

The European Union funds innovation and tech entrepreneurship through various programs open to African startups and entrepreneurs, including partnerships with Liberian innovators.

What's Funded

  • Innovation projects
  • Tech development

Key Requirements

  • Partnership with EU entity sometimes required
