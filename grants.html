U.S. Embassy Monrovia

Self-Help & Democracy Small Grants

NGO / CSO Open

U.S. Embassy Self-Help (SSH) and Democracy & Human Rights (DHRA) small grants for registered Liberian nonprofits and community organizations. Supports grassroots development and civic programs.

💰Amount:$5,000 – $10,000
🎯Focus:Community development, democracy, human rights
✅Eligible:Registered Liberian NGOs & community groups
📅Cycle:Annual — check lr.usembassy.gov for current calls
Apply Directly ↗ Get TIH Help Up to $10K

USAID Liberia

Agribusiness Incubator & Co-Financing Grant

Business / MSME Agriculture Open

USAID Liberia's MSME Business Incubator provides co-financing and technical support to agribusiness entrepreneurs across rice, cassava, fisheries, and poultry value chains. Includes business plan development and market linkages.

💰Amount:Up to $50,000 co-financing
🎯Focus:Agribusiness, value chains, rural entrepreneurs
✅Eligible:Liberian agribusiness owners & entrepreneurs
📅Deadline:Rolling — check usaid.gov/liberia for current calls
Apply Directly ↗ Get TIH Help Up to $50K

European Union Delegation — Liberia

Civil Society & Environmental Governance Grant

NGO / CSO Open 2025–2026

EU grant facility supporting Liberian civil society organizations and NGOs working on governance, environmental protection, and transparency. €2.35M total envelope across two lots for multi-year programs.

💰Amount:€1.1M – €1.175M per lot (total €2.35M)
🎯Focus:Governance, environment, accountability
✅Eligible:Registered CSOs, NGOs, civil society coalitions
📅Cycle:2025–2026 call (check EU Delegation Monrovia)
Apply Directly ↗ Get TIH Help €1.1M+

African Development Bank (AfDB)

Youth Entrepreneurship Investment Bank (YEIB)

Youth Business / MSME Agriculture Starting 2026

AfDB's $17M Youth Entrepreneurship Investment Bank targets youth-led agribusinesses and MSMEs in Liberia. Provides grants, blended finance, and technical assistance to entrepreneurs aged 18–35, with special focus on women-led enterprises.

💰Amount:$17M program — individual grants vary
🎯Focus:Youth agribusiness, SMEs, women entrepreneurs
✅Eligible:Liberian youth ages 18–35, registered MSMEs
📅Launch:Operations starting early 2026
Learn More ↗ Get TIH Help $17M Pool

World Bank / WARDIP2

West Africa Regional Digital Integration Program 2

Digital / Tech Business / MSME Open 2025–2027

World Bank's $137M regional digital program (WARDIP2) Liberia component supports digital SMEs, tech startups, and digital entrepreneurs. Provides grants and capacity building for firms developing digital products and services.

💰Amount:Part of $137M regional program
🎯Focus:Digital technology, ICT, tech startups
✅Eligible:Digital SMEs & tech startups in Liberia
📅Cycle:2025–2027 ongoing implementation
Learn More ↗ Get TIH Help $137M Pool

Liberia LIFT Project (World Bank)

MSME Line of Credit — Phase II

Business / MSME Agriculture Open

LIFT Project Phase II provides a $3.5M line of credit and grants to Liberian-owned small businesses in agriculture, manufacturing, and technology that cannot access conventional bank financing.

💰Amount:$3.5M total facility — individual amounts vary
🎯Focus:Agriculture, manufacturing, technology SMEs
✅Eligible:Liberian-owned businesses without bank access
📅Status:Phase II — accepting applications via MoCI
Apply via MoCI ↗ Get TIH Help $3.5M Pool

UNDP Liberia + Ministry of Commerce & Industry

MSME Growth Accelerator Program — Cohort 5

Business / MSME Youth Open — Cohort 5

UNDP Liberia and MoCI MSME Growth Accelerator delivers capacity building, mentorship, and seed funding for Liberian micro, small, and medium enterprises. Cohort 5 launched July 2025 and is actively accepting participants.

💰Amount:Capacity building + seed funding
🎯Focus:Business development, entrepreneurship, mentorship
✅Eligible:Liberian MSMEs at any stage
📅Status:Cohort 5 open — launched July 2025
Apply via UNDP ↗ Get TIH Help Seed + Training

Friends of Liberia (FoL)

Small Grants for Liberian Nonprofits

NGO / CSO Rolling Basis

Friends of Liberia offers small grants to registered Liberian nonprofit organizations for community health, education, and social development projects. Applications accepted year-round on a rolling basis.

💰Amount:Up to $3,000 per grant
🎯Focus:Health, education, community development
✅Eligible:Registered Liberian nonprofit organizations
📅Deadline:Rolling — apply any time at fol.org
Apply at FoL ↗ Get TIH Help Up to $3K

Government of Canada

Canada Fund for Local Initiatives (CFLI)

NGO / CSO Business Open

Canada's CFLI provides small-to-medium grants to NGOs, businesses, academic institutions, and private-sector organizations in Liberia for projects advancing inclusive growth, gender equality, environment, and governance.

💰Amount:CAD $15,000 – $100,000 per project
🎯Focus:Inclusive growth, gender equality, governance
✅Eligible:NGOs, businesses, academics, private sector
📅Cycle:Annual — via Canadian High Commission Accra
Apply via Canada ↗ Get TIH Help CAD $15K–$100K
🏆

Ministry of Commerce & Industry + World Bank

National MSME Conference Cash Awards

Business / MSME Agriculture Annual

MoCI Liberia and World Bank award cash grants at the annual National MSME Conference to top-performing small businesses and farmers selected through a competitive review of business plans, growth impact, and job creation.

💰Amount:$1,200 – $5,000 per winner
🎯Focus:Business growth, agriculture, job creation
✅Eligible:Liberian MSMEs and farmers — formal or informal
📅Cycle:Annual — National MSME Conference
Apply via MoCI ↗ Get TIH Help $1.2K–$5K
