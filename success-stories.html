Moses S.: From Monrovia to International Studies

Moses grew up in Monrovia, the son of a market trader and a schoolteacher. He was a strong student but had no roadmap for how to pursue higher education beyond Liberia's borders. When he first heard about international study opportunities in India, he thought it was out of reach. Too many forms, too many requirements, and no one in his community who had done it before.

He found Tolbert Innovation Hub through a friend's recommendation and came in for an eligibility assessment. Our team walked him through every requirement of the application: the documents he needed, the statement of purpose format, the academic transcript certification, and the language proficiency pathway. We helped him register for IELTS preparation through our free classroom. He accessed every module, completed all practice tests, and logged more than 40 hours of study time on the platform.

When his IELTS score came back, Moses had cleared the threshold. We guided him through the final stages of his application, helped him prepare his admission letter request, and supported him through the visa documentation process. Eight months after walking through our doors for the first time, Moses departed Monrovia for New Delhi to begin his undergraduate studies in Computer Science.

Outcome: Moses is now in his second year of a Bachelor of Technology program in India, maintaining a GPA above 3.5 and mentoring other Liberian students remotely through our classroom platform.

When Fatima's mother was diagnosed with a condition requiring specialist cardiac care not available in Liberia, the family felt lost. Our Healthcare Exposure team helped them identify appropriate facilities in India, coordinated with their local physician for a referral letter, assisted with medical visa documentation, and provided logistical guidance.
