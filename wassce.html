What is WASSCE?

The West African Senior School Certificate Exam

The WASSCE (West African Senior School Certificate Examination) is the final secondary school examination in Liberia, conducted by the West African Examinations Council (WAEC). It is taken by Form 6 / Grade 12 students and is the gateway to university admission in Liberia and across West Africa.

In Liberia, WAEC operates two sittings annually: the May/June series (the main sitting for school candidates) and the November/December series (for private candidates re-sitting).

  • 🏛️Conductor: West African Examinations Council (WAEC), Monrovia Regional Office
  • 📅Main sitting: May – June (school candidates)
  • 📅Re-sit: November – December (private candidates)
  • 🎓Required for: University of Liberia, Cuttington University, and all accredited Liberian universities
  • 📋Minimum requirement: Credit (A1–C6) in at least 5 subjects including English and Maths
  • 💰Registration fee: Paid through WAEC-approved schools and registration centers

Core Subject — All Candidates

English Language

Core Paper 1, 2 & 3

English Language is compulsory for every WASSCE candidate. It tests essay writing, letter writing, comprehension, summary, oral English, and vocabulary — across 3 papers totalling 200 marks.

Key Topics to Master

Argumentative Essay Narrative / Descriptive Essay Formal Letter Informal Letter Speech / Report Writing Comprehension Passage Summary Writing Lexis & Structure (OBJ) Oral English (Phonetics)
TIH Tip: Practice writing one timed essay every week. For comprehension, always answer in your own words. The oral/phonetics paper is often the easiest — memorize vowel and consonant sounds early.

Core Subject — All Candidates

Mathematics (Core)

Core Paper 1 & 2

Core Mathematics is mandatory for all candidates. Paper 1 is 40-question objective (multiple choice). Paper 2 is essay/theory where you show working. Both papers test number theory, algebra, geometry, statistics, and trigonometry.

Key Topics to Master

Number & Numeration Algebra (equations, inequalities) Mensuration (areas, volumes) Geometry (triangles, circles) Trigonometry (sin, cos, tan) Statistics (mean, median, mode) Probability Matrices & Transformation Locus & Construction
TIH Tip: Never skip showing your working — partial marks are awarded in Paper 2. For Paper 1, eliminate wrong options by substituting values. Past WAEC questions repeat patterns every 3–4 years.

Science Track — Elective

Biology

Science Paper 1, 2 & Practical

Biology covers life processes, cell biology, genetics, ecology, evolution, and plant/animal systems. The practical paper tests laboratory skills and specimen identification — a common area where students lose marks.

Key Topics to Master

Cell Structure & Organisation Nutrition in Plants & Animals Photosynthesis & Respiration Reproduction (Sexual & Asexual) Genetics & Heredity (Mendel) Ecology & Food Chains Evolution & Adaptation Transport in Plants & Animals Excretion & Homeostasis
TIH Tip: Draw and label diagrams neatly — Biology rewards neat, labeled diagrams. Know the difference between mitosis and meiosis. For Genetics: always show your Punnett square in cross-breeding questions.

Science Track — Elective

Chemistry

Science Paper 1, 2 & Practical

Chemistry is tested in Inorganic, Organic, and Physical Chemistry sections. The practical paper involves acid-base titrations, identification tests, and observation recording — students often underperform here due to poor lab preparation.

Key Topics to Master

Atomic Structure & Bonding Periodic Table Trends Acids, Bases & Salts Redox Reactions Electrolysis Organic Chemistry (Alkanes, Alkenes) Mole Concept & Stoichiometry Chemical Equilibrium (Le Chatelier) Industrial Chemistry
TIH Tip: Memorize the activity series and solubility rules — they come up every year. For organic chemistry, know how to draw structural formulas. Titration calculations follow a fixed pattern — learn it once.

Science Track — Elective

Physics

Science Paper 1, 2 & Practical

Physics covers mechanics, waves, electricity, magnetism, optics, and modern physics. Calculation-heavy subjects where formula application and unit analysis are critical. The practical requires drawing and analysing graphs from data tables.

Key Topics to Master

Mechanics (Newton's Laws) Work, Energy & Power Waves & Sound Light & Optics Electricity & Circuits Magnetism & Electromagnetism Heat & Temperature Radioactivity & Nuclear Physics Gravitational Field
TIH Tip: Always write your formula before substituting values. Memorize SI units — wrong units lose marks. Graph questions in Paper 3: use a ruler, plot all points, draw a line of best fit, and state the gradient clearly.

Arts / Business Track — Elective

Economics

Arts Business Paper 1 & 2

Economics is one of the most popular WASSCE electives. It covers microeconomics (supply, demand, markets) and macroeconomics (national income, monetary policy, trade). Paper 2 requires long essay answers — structured arguments score best.

Key Topics to Master

Demand & Supply Elasticity (PED, PES, YED) Market Structures (Monopoly, etc.) National Income (GDP, GNP) Money & Banking Fiscal & Monetary Policy International Trade & Balance of Payments Public Finance (Taxes, Budget) Economic Development (Liberia context)
TIH Tip: Always define key terms before answering essay questions. Use real Liberian examples (CBL, LBDI, LEC, LPRC) — examiners appreciate local context. Draw demand/supply curves clearly with labeled axes.

Arts Track — Elective

Literature in English

Arts Paper 1, 2 & 3

Literature tests your understanding of prose, drama, poetry, and oral literature. Paper 1 is objective (literary devices, unseen passages). Papers 2 and 3 are essays on set texts — understanding themes, characters, and style is key.

Key Topics to Master

Literary Devices (metaphor, irony, etc.) Prose Analysis (set texts) Drama / Play Analysis Poetry (imagery, tone, theme) Oral Literature (folktales, proverbs) Characterisation Theme & Conflict Unseen Passage Criticism
TIH Tip: Read the complete set texts — do not rely on summaries alone. Quote directly from the text in your essays. Practice identifying literary devices in past questions. Oral literature questions often relate to proverbs used in the text.

Arts / Social Science Track — Elective

Government / Civic Education

Arts Paper 1 & 2

Government covers political theory, constitutional law, comparative government, and international relations. Questions on Liberia's constitution, ECOWAS, AU, and UN are very common — especially for Liberian candidates.

Key Topics to Master

Liberian Constitution 1986 Branches of Government Political Parties & Elections ECOWAS, AU, UN Types of Government (Democracy, etc.) Human Rights Federalism vs Unitarism Legislature, Executive, Judiciary Public Administration & Bureaucracy
TIH Tip: Know the specific articles of the Liberian Constitution — Article 1, 2, 34 (Legislature), 50 (Executive) are commonly tested. For ECOWAS questions, know founding year (1975), headquarters (Abuja), and member states (15).

Geography

Arts Paper 1, 2 & Practical

Geography covers physical geography (weather, climate, landforms), human geography (population, agriculture, trade), regional geography (West Africa, Liberia), and map reading / practical skills. Map-reading questions are worth significant marks.

Key Topics to Master

Weather & Climate (tropical) Landforms (rivers, coasts, rocks) Population & Settlement Agriculture (types, crops) Industry & Energy Resources West Africa Regional Geography Liberia's Geography (counties, rivers) Topographic Map Reading Environmental Issues
TIH Tip: Practice reading topographic maps — identify contours, spot heights, and calculate gradient. Know Liberia's 15 counties, their capitals, and major rivers (St. Paul, Cavalla, St. John). Climate graphs appear almost every year.

Business / Commercial Track — Elective

Financial Accounting

Business Paper 1 & 2

Financial Accounting covers bookkeeping, double-entry system, T-accounts, trial balance, trading and profit/loss accounts, balance sheets, and partnership accounts. Accuracy in figures and account formats earns full marks.

Key Topics to Master

Double Entry & Bookkeeping Trial Balance Trading Account Profit & Loss Account Balance Sheet Depreciation (SLM, RBM) Partnership Accounts Bank Reconciliation Incomplete Records
TIH Tip: The Trading, P&L, and Balance Sheet appear EVERY year — master the format completely. Always start with the Trial Balance. When totals don't balance, check for transposition errors (e.g., 36 written as 63).

Business / Commercial Track — Elective

Commerce

Business Paper 1 & 2

Commerce covers trade, business organizations, banking, insurance, transport, warehousing, and communication in business. Strong theory-based subject where knowing definitions and classifications earns marks.

Key Topics to Master

Types of Trade (wholesale, retail) Business Organisations (Ltd, Co-op, etc.) Banking & Financial Services Insurance (principles, types) Transport & Communication Advertising & Promotion Import & Export Trade Documents in Trade (invoice, L/C) Consumer Protection
TIH Tip: Distinguish clearly between a sole trader, partnership, limited company, and cooperative — exam questions often confuse these. For insurance, memorize the 6 principles: indemnity, insurable interest, utmost good faith, proximate cause, subrogation, contribution.

🎯 Proven WAEC Strategies

10 Strategies to Score Higher on WASSCE

These strategies are based on WAEC chief examiner reports and the experience of TIH tutors who have helped hundreds of Liberian students pass their WASSCE.

01

Start Past Questions Early

WAEC repeats topics every 3–4 years. Solving past questions from 2015–2025 reveals patterns and boosts confidence. Practice under exam time conditions.

02

Read the Syllabus, Not Just Textbooks

Download the official WAEC Liberia syllabus for each subject. Topics outside the syllabus are never tested — focus your time on what counts.

03

Understand, Don't Just Memorize

WAEC questions test application, not rote memory. Understand WHY formulas work and you'll be able to solve variations you've never seen before.

04

Attempt All Questions (Where Allowed)

In objective papers, never leave blanks — there's no negative marking in most WAEC subjects. Educated guesses on MCQ can earn marks.

05

Time Your Papers Strictly

In a 2.5-hour paper with 5 essays, that's 30 minutes per question. Practice timed mock exams so you stop over-writing on early questions.

06

Answer What You Know First

In theory papers, answer your strongest questions first to build momentum and secure early marks. Return to harder questions with remaining time.

07

Write Legibly & Structure Answers

Examiners mark dozens of scripts. Use paragraphs, bold key terms (where allowed), and clear headings for essay questions to make marking easy.

08

Review WAEC Chief Examiner Reports

WAEC publishes Chief Examiner Reports after each exam listing common mistakes. Read them — they tell you exactly what loses marks each year.

09

Form a Study Group (Max 4 People)

Teaching a topic to a peer is the fastest way to know if you truly understand it. Keep groups small and focused — social groups don't study well.

10

Sleep Well the Night Before

Memory consolidation happens during sleep. An all-nighter before the exam impairs recall. Sleep 7–8 hours, eat breakfast, and arrive at the hall 30 minutes early.
